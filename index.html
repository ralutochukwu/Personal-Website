Solid State Synthesis and Computational Modeling Internship Funded by NNSA

The summer research internship was focused on the study of a family of ordered oxyfluorides, Sr3- 2xCex AlO4F (Host Structure) where RE stands for rare earth metals. (Rare earth ions are known to emit light at known wavelengths) The goal was to determine the amount of Ce+3 substitution that will produce the greatest photoluminescence intensity. Additionally, we also viewed computational models of molecules of oxyfluoride compounds using python.
